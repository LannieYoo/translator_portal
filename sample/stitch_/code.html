dictionary

/ˈdɪkʃəˌnɛri/

Noun

1.

a book or electronic resource that lists the words of a language (typically in alphabetical order) and gives their meaning, or gives the equivalent words in a different language, often also providing information about pronunciation, origin, and usage.

“I'll look up 'rhubarb' in the dictionary.”

2.

a reference book on any subject, the items of which are arranged in alphabetical order.

“a dictionary of quotations”

Synonyms

wordbook vocabulary list lexicon glossary

Origin

Mid 16th century: from medieval Latin dictionarium (manuale) or liber dictionarius ‘manual or book of words’, from Latin dictio ‘word, saying’, from dicere ‘to say’.
